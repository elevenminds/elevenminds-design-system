Pairing rules

Don't break these on dark

Lift the accent, not the surface

If the brand's primary fails AA on Bg 1, lift the brand color — not the background. Surfaces stay on the four-step neutral stack across every sub-system.

Never pure black

The deepest surface is #0A0A0C. True #000 reads as a hole, not a surface, and breaks shadow / glow effects.

Alert ladders saturate, not lighten

NAANIX's six-color alert ladder shifts toward warmer, more saturated stops on dark — not pastel. Severity must still feel urgent at a glance.

Glow is a SphereGT thing

Cyan glows, pulsing pins, gradient orbs — these belong to SphereGT. Don't import them into NAANIX or LinkerWeb on dark; each brand keeps its surface vocabulary.

Tables stay ruled

LinkerWeb's borders thin to #2A2A33 but never disappear. The "infrastructure" feel comes from explicit lines — even on dark.

One brand per surface

Don't mix sub-system accents on a single screen. A NAANIX dispatch view stays Naanix-red; a SphereGT dashboard stays cyan. Cross-brand chrome (this page) is the exception.
